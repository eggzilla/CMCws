General Information

The information below is both a tutorial and a description of of the webserver. Tooltips are positioned at critical junctions in the workflow, please use them as well. If you have additional suggestions, questions or comments please do not hesitate to contact us.

Input

The first step is to choose one of the 2 types of comparison offered by CMCws (see Figure 1) and upload a set of models (Figure 2).

Type 1: covariance model vs Rfam compares each of the provided covariance models against all covariance models in available in Rfam 11.
Type 2: covariance model vs covariance model compare each of the provided covariance models with each other.

Figure 1 Figure 2

Uploaded files (see Figure 2) can contain up to 10 covariance models or stockholm alignments and must not exeed 1 megabyte in size. covariance model vs covariance model comparison requires at least 2 models.

The second step lets you review your input before submission. If you want to compare your model against Rfam (Figure 3) you can additionally specify the entry type of model, e.g. miRNA (see Rfam User-manual). All other models will be omitted.
In case of comparing a set of models with each other just a summary of submitted models is displayed (Figure 4).

Figure 3 Figure 4
Clicking Compare takes you to the processing step.
